Reseller vs. Owner

The Reseller Dilemma

You own the customer relationship, but you don't own the asset. The vendor dictates the rules, the price, and the features.

The Old Way

Traditional Reselling

  • Low Margins: You keep 10-20% of the license fee, while the vendor takes the lion's share.
  • No Control: Your customers ask for features, but you have to wait for the vendor's roadmap - which might never align with your niche needs.
  • No IP Ownership: You are selling someone else's product. You build no equity in the software.
  • Vendor Lock-in: If the vendor raises prices or changes strategy, your business is at risk.
  • Competition: You are selling the exact same SKU as 10 other partners in your region.
The BW2 Way

Value-Added Partnership

  • High Margins: Keep 100% of your service and customization revenue.
  • Full Control: Define your own pricing model and manage your own features roadmap.
  • IP Ownership: You own the customized solutions you build. You become the vendor.
  • Customer Sticky-ness: The customization layer allows you to implement specific, critical requirements of individual clients that generic software can't touch.
  • Differentiation: Sell a unique solution tailored to your industry niche, not a commodity.
